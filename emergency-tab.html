What to Do in a Mental Health Crisis

Prioritize Safety:

  • If you are in immediate danger to yourself or others, call emergency services or go to the nearest emergency room.
  • If possible, remove yourself from any potentially dangerous situations.
  • If you have a safety plan, follow it.

Reach Out for Help:

  • Call a crisis hotline or suicide prevention lifeline. These resources are available 24/7 and provide confidential support.
  • Contact a trusted friend, family member, or mental health professional.
  • If you are already working with a therapist or psychiatrist, contact them immediately.

Use Grounding Techniques:

  • These techniques can help you stay present and manage overwhelming emotions.

Focus on Your Breathing:

  • Practice deep, slow breaths. Inhale deeply through your nose, hold for a few seconds, and exhale slowly through your mouth. Kindly visit our Discovey page for more a detailed guide on breathing techniques.

Engage Your Senses:

  • 5-4-3-2-1 Method: Name five things you can see, four things you can touch, three things you can hear, two things you can smell, and one thing you can taste.
  • Hold a cold object, such as an ice cube.
  • Listen to calming music or nature sounds.
  • Focus on the sensation of your feet on the ground.

Practice Self-Soothing:

  • Wrap yourself in a blanket.
  • Drink a warm beverage.
  • Engage in a calming activity, such as reading or drawing.

Remember:

  • These feelings are temporary.
  • Help is available.
  • You are not alone.

Grounding Techniques

The 5-4-3-2-1 Method:

This technique involves using your senses to bring you back to the present moment.

  • 5 things you can see.
  • 4 things you can touch.
  • 3 things you can hear.
  • 2 things you can smell.
  • 1 thing you can taste.

Deep Breathing:

  • Slow, deep breaths can help regulate your heart rate and calm your nervous system.

Physical Grounding:

  • Focus on the sensation of your feet on the ground.
  • Hold a comforting object, such as a soft blanket or a smooth stone.
  • Run cool water over your hands.

Mental Grounding:

  • Repeat a calming phrase or affirmation.
  • Visualize a safe and peaceful place.
  • Engage in a simple mental task, such as counting backward.
